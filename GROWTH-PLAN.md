## Headline expectation

Realistic horizon under Path B (the default until Oct 15, 2026): **24–36
months** to 5k–15k active users, possibly 50k+ if a catalyst hits but
plannable around 10k. Under Path A (if scale-decision goes that way): 24–30
months to 50k+. Three things compound and have to run in parallel:

1. **Founder voice** — Jimmy's writing as a public asset (Substack, recast from the five-publication queue)
2. **Product virality** — the redesigned quiz reveal as a passive acquisition engine
3. **Community embedding** — becoming a known voice in adjacent communities (anglophone + Mainland)

None of these alone gets to 50k. Together they do, slowly.

---

## Path A / Path B / Path C framework

The Oct 15, 2026 scale-or-maintain decision determines which version of
this plan runs. Until then, **plan for Path B so a Path A decision is
upside, not scramble.**

**Path A — SCALE.** Mull becomes primary career bet. Founder track replaces
operator track. Master's becomes optional / part-time / deferred. The
original (May 12) plan applies at full intensity: weekly Substack, 2–3
TikTok/Reels per week, friends/circles ship Q1 2027, influencer outreach
campaign, all of it.

**Path B — MAINTAIN.** Mull stays as differentiation artefact alongside
master's track. Time budget: 6–10 hrs/week outside academic terms, 0–2
hrs/week during exam windows and master's application crunch. **This is
the version this document defaults to.**

**Path C — WIND DOWN.** Not expected, but acknowledged. The product
stays live; active development stops. This plan doesn't apply.

Decision criteria at Oct 15, 2026: v1.0 ships, 30 days of post-launch
data, retention + Mull+ conversion + organic growth signals reviewed
against the pivot triggers below.

## Phase 0 · Foundation (now → Week 6)

**Goal:** Ship a product ready to receive users at scale. Set up owned
distribution. Stop optimizing for 50 friends; start optimizing for the
trajectory.

### Product
- **Redesign migration** (~3–4 weeks, in progress via Claude Code).
- **Push notifications** via PWA service worker — daily dilemma push at user's chosen hour.
- **iOS / Android home-screen widget** — today's dilemma + your archetype.
- **Onboarding flow polish** within the redesign — the first 60 seconds after a new user finishes the quiz determines whether they come back.
- **Email capture on the current site immediately** — even a single "I'll let you know when the new version is ready" form. Ship this week. Basic, undone, free.

### Owned distribution setup
- **Start the Substack now** at **bi-weekly cadence** (not weekly). Working title *The Operator-Philosopher* or similar. ~2–4 hours per piece. **The five named publications from the broader trajectory (Engelsberg op-ed, Chinese-language op-ed, Society Journal piece, RIP essay, Bagehot piece) double-publish as Substack issues — don't write original content on top, recast and repurpose.**
- **Write the first 3 posts** before going public:
  1. *"Why I delayed Mull's launch"* — the post you already drafted, expanded
  2. *"What I mean when I say a 16-dimensional model"* — the methodology, accessibly explained. **This becomes the lay-summary of the faculty co-authored paper using Mull data (Creative Leverage Move #3).**
  3. *"The day I realized Hume and Buddha agreed about something"* — substantive philosophical essay that demonstrates intellectual seriousness without selling
- **Set up analytics:** Plausible or Fathom (privacy-respecting). Vercel Analytics is fine, but a separate funnel tool helps.

### Community presence (lurk mode, not promote mode)
- **Join and read** for at least 30 days before contributing: r/philosophy, r/Stoicism, r/askphilosophy, LessWrong, 2–3 Discord philosophy servers, philosophy Substack writers.
- **Make a list of 30 newsletter writers / podcasters / YouTubers / academics** whose audience overlaps. Don't email yet — read them, study them. This list is a strategic asset for Y3.

### Recurring habits to establish now
- **Bi-weekly Substack post** — same day every 2 weeks. 600–1,000 words. Quality > consistency, but both matter.
- **1–2 thoughtful Reddit / Substack comments per week** during terms (not daily). More during breaks if energy permits.
- **Friday Mull metrics review (30 min/week)** — signups, retention, daily-dilemma-completion rate. Even at small numbers, build the habit.

## Phase 1 · Soft relaunch (Week 6 → Week 10)

**Goal:** 200–500 active users. Validate retention. Learn.

### Launch sequence
- **Week 6:** Friends + IG/TikTok announce. Use the LinkedIn/IG drafts. Personal note to the 30–50 original friends.
- **Weeks 7–10:** Iterate based on feedback. Fix what's clearly broken. **Don't add new features.**
- **Substack:** keep posting bi-weekly. 100–300 subscribers by end of phase is realistic if you've shared posts on existing IG/LinkedIn.

### Community embedding begins
- **Reddit:** 30 days lurked. Start commenting on relevant posts. Maybe one post per week of your own — thoughtful question, not Mull pitch.
- **Substack engagement:** Comment on 2–3 philosophy writers' posts per week. Email 1 with a genuine note (no ask, just appreciation).

### Metrics to watch
- **Day-7 retention** (% of users back a week later) — should be 25%+. Below 15% means the product loop is broken; reassess before scaling acquisition.
- **Quiz completion rate** — should be 75%+. Below 60% = onboarding broken.
- **Share rate** — % of users who screenshot or share their result.

### Phase exit criteria
- Day-7 retention ≥ 25%
- 5+ qualitative reports of "I tell my friends about this"
- Substack at 150+ subscribers
- 2–3 things identified that resonate (specific dilemmas, archetypes, features) — these become content angles

## Phase 2 · Public relaunch (Month 3 → Month 4)

**Goal:** 800–2.5k users. First wave of strangers. Build content cadence.

### Launch moments (one per week, spaced)

**Show HN.** Title: "Show HN: Mull — a 16-dimensional model of how you
think." Post Tuesday morning EST. **Expect the median outcome: 200–1k
visitors, 5–50 signups.** The 5k–15k visitor / 200–800 signup figure cited
in the original plan is the 90th percentile result. Schedule it for
completeness; don't bet the quarter on it.

**Product Hunt.** Separate week. Post early morning Pacific. Same
median-expectation framing.

**Press pitch.** Short pitch email to 2–3 tech journalists who cover indie +
philosophy + tech. Specific person preferred. Not bulk.

**First podcast appearance.** Pitch yourself to 5 podcasts in the
philosophy + indie hacker + thoughtful-tech intersection. **Realistic
landing rate: 1 in 5.** Aim for 1 appearance in this window.

### TikTok / Reels content (Path B: deferred. Path A: only if chosen)
- **Under Path B: defer indefinitely.** Time cost is incompatible with Y2 academic load. 30–60 sec founder-on-camera clip = 2–3 hours of scripting + filming + editing. The "2–3 per week" cadence from the original plan = 4–9 hrs/week, which Y2 can't absorb.
- **Under Path A (if chosen Oct 15):** pick ONE platform (TikTok or Reels, not both). 1 post/week cadence.

### Mull+ subscription stub goes live
- ~$8/month, $60/year. Conservative pricing.
- **Realistic conversion at 50k users: 1–2%, not 5%.** Philosophy apps with high free utility convert lower than the original plan assumed. Project Mull+ revenue at 50k users: $4–8k MRR, not $20k MRR.

### Phase exit criteria
- 1.5k+ active users (took an action in past 7 days)
- Newsletter at 500+ subscribers
- 1 podcast appearance recorded or scheduled

## Phase 3 · Habit + feature depth (Month 4 → Month 9)

**Goal:** 2k–6k users. Lock in retention. Mull+ revenue begins covering
infrastructure costs.

### Major product launches

**Year-end retrospective.** **Hard deadline: Dec 31, 2026.** Free,
animated, beautifully shareable. Spotify Wrapped pattern. ~1 week dev + 2
weeks design budget. One annual moment that drives sharing.

**Enriched philosopher pages.** Roll out 30/month (down from the original
plan's 50/month — bandwidth-realistic). Each page: 300–500 word intro, 3–5
quotes, "if you score high on X, read this." SEO long-tail compounds for
years.

**Friends/circles feature.** **Path A only.** Highest single
retention/virality lever for products that succeed with it — but
expectation for philosophy is moderated. Most people share "my Spotify
Wrapped" (low-vulnerability identity signal). Few share "my 16-dimensional
philosophical worldview" (closer to therapy notes). Worth shipping under
Path A; backlog under Path B.

**Voice diary** (audio entries with transcription). Path A only. 1 week of
work, decent retention impact if shipped.

### Marketing rhythm
- **Substack at 800–2.5k subscribers** by end of phase. Quality consistent.
- **TikTok/Reels:** Path B = none, Path A = stable cadence (1/week).
- **No major influencer outreach yet.** Defer to Y3.

### Phase exit criteria (Path B)
- 4k+ active users
- 50+ Mull+ subscribers (~$400 MRR; covers Anthropic API + Vercel + Supabase)
- Newsletter 1.5–3k subscribers
- Year-end retrospective shipped and screenshotted by users

## Phase 5 · Network compounding (Y3+)

**Path B realistic ceiling:** 10–25k users over Y3, possibly 50k+ if
catalyst hits. **Path A target:** 30k–50k+ users over Y3.

### What changes at this scale
- **Network effects mature.** Each new user brings 0.2–0.4 more users via
  share-quiz / circle invites (under Path A) / organic word-of-mouth.
- **Founder brand established.** Substack 6–15k subs by end of Y3 under
  Path B; possibly 15k+ under Path A.
- **Mull+ funds operations modestly.** Under Path B at 10k users with 1.5%
  conversion: 150 paid subs × $8 = $1.2k MRR / $14k ARR. Not life-changing
  but covers ops. Under Path A at 50k users with 2% conversion: 1k paid
  subs × $8 = $8k MRR / $96k ARR.
- **Capacitor App Store wrap: not before Y3.** Premature in Y2.

### Maintenance mode for marketing
- Substack continues, possibly slowed to monthly if other work demands.
- Community engagement: light touch maintains recognized-voice status.
- Reassess Path A vs Path B annually at scale-or-maintain checkpoints.

## Pivot triggers

When to reconsider.

- **Day-7 retention drops below 15% post-relaunch:** product loop is broken, not a marketing problem. Stop scaling acquisition. Iterate product.
- **Substack stuck under 300 subscribers after 12 bi-weekly posts (6 months):** voice/topic mismatch. Test 2–3 different formats.
- **Show HN flops (under 50 upvotes):** framing is wrong. Don't keep retrying HN; pivot.
- **No podcast pitches landing after 15 sent:** pitch quality is wrong. Get a critique.
- **18 months in, still under 2k users:** channel mix isn't working. Honest reassessment — possibly bring in a paid acquisition test ($500/month experiment) for the first time.
- **Oct 15, 2026 data shows Day-7 retention <20% AND Mull+ conversion <0.5%:** the scale-or-maintain decision likely tilts toward Path C (wind down development).

## What to deliberately NOT do

- **Paid ads** (until 24+ months in, if ever). Performance ads for niche consumer = brutal CAC.
- **Influencer pay-for-post deals.** Cheapens the brand; audience smells it.
- **Mass cold email outreach.** Don't.
- **Generic startup growth hacks** (referral pyramid schemes, fake scarcity, exit-intent popups). Misaligned with Mull's voice.
- **Native app development** before PWA proves out. Premature.
- **Excessive A/B testing** at low volume. Won't reach significance.
- **Trying to be on every platform.** Path B: Substack + Reddit + (later) WeChat. Skip everything else. Path A adds one short-form video platform.
- **Original Substack content on top of the five-publication queue.** Recast and repurpose.
- **Maria Popova / Tim Ferriss / Ezra Klein outreach in Y2.** Defer to Y3 after Bagehot Prize.
- **TikTok / Reels at 2–3/week.** Time-cost incompatible with Y2 unless Path A.
- **Friends/circles feature before Oct 15 decision.** Backlog under Path B.

## Decision point: Oct 15, 2026

The scale-or-maintain decision drives which version of this plan runs from
Y2 spring onward.

### Decision inputs
- 30 days of v1.0 post-launch data
- Day-7 retention (Path A green-light if ≥30%; yellow if 20–30%; red if <20%)
- Mull+ conversion rate (Path A green-light if ≥2%; yellow if 1–2%; red if <1%)
- Organic week-over-week growth (Path A green-light if ≥10%/week; yellow if 3–10%; red if <3%)
- Founder energy + bandwidth assessment
- Master's track standing (faculty cultivation progress, GPA, publication queue)

### Decision outcomes
- **All green + founder wants it: Path A.** Switch to original plan's full intensity. Mull becomes primary career bet.
- **Mixed signals or founder ambivalent: Path B.** Continue scaled-back plan. Reassess at next checkpoint.
- **Multiple red signals: Path C.** Wind down active development. Keep product live as portfolio piece.

### Prep before Oct 15
- Year-end retrospective shipped (the data it generates feeds the decision)
- Mull+ live for at least 60 days (gives conversion data)
- Substack has 6 months of cadence (gives founder-voice signal)
- Initial Mainland lurk-and-listen complete (gives Path A Mainland-readiness signal)

## The unfair truth

A 50k-user niche consumer product is a craft project, not a growth-hacking
project. The founders who hit this scale aren't the cleverest marketers —
they're the ones who keep writing, keep building, keep showing up, for two
to four years. Most attempts plateau at 2k–5k users because the founder
loses patience or pivots prematurely.

The single highest-leverage thing within Jimmy's control is **committing to
two years of bi-weekly Substack posts before judging whether marketing is
working.** Bigger cadence promises that don't hold are worse than smaller
cadence promises that do. The bi-weekly cadence (recasting from the
five-publication queue) is the version Jimmy can actually deliver across
Y2 + Y3.

Everything else on this plan supports that core habit. If the writing
compounds, the rest follows. If it doesn't, the rest won't save it.

Mull has the product depth, the brand voice, and the founder to be in the
small set of niche consumer products that work. Whether it reaches 50k is
mostly a function of (a) whether Jimmy stays in the saddle for 24+ months
and (b) whether the Oct 15, 2026 scale-or-maintain decision unlocks the
bandwidth to push past Path B's natural ceiling.
